New York — The City That Never Sleeps

Skyscrapers, Times Square, Central Park, iconic landmarks and world-class food scenes.

USA Best Time: April–June / Sept–Nov City • Skyline • Culture

Quick Overview

New York blends towering skyscrapers, beautiful parks, historic neighborhoods, modern art museums, and world-famous restaurants. From Times Square’s bright nights to peaceful Central Park mornings — NYC is pure cinematic magic.

  • Times Square — neon heart of NYC
  • Central Park — nature in the middle of skyscrapers
  • Statue of Liberty & Ellis Island
  • Brooklyn Bridge — iconic sunrise/sunset walk
  • Empire State & Rockefeller Center
  • The MET & MoMA museums

3-Day Best Itinerary

Day 1 — Times Square, Broadway & Midtown

Start at Times Square, visit Rockefeller Center, St. Patrick’s Cathedral, then head to the Empire State Building. Evening: Broadway lights walk.

Day 2 — Statue of Liberty & Lower Manhattan

Morning ferry to Liberty Island & Ellis Island museum. Afternoon: explore Wall Street, 9/11 Memorial, walk Brooklyn Bridge at sunset.

Day 3 — Central Park & Museums

Relax in Central Park, row boats at the lake, then visit either MET or MoMA. Evening: Times Square night photography.

Tips for NYC

  • Use subway — fastest way around.
  • Carry a MetroCard or tap-to-pay.
  • Book Statue of Liberty tickets early.
  • Stay alert in crowds (tourist areas are busy).
  • Try New York pizza & cheesecake.
