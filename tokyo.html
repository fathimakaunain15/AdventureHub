Tokyo — Neon Nights & Timeless Culture

Shibuya crossing, ancient temples, anime streets of Akihabara, cherry blossoms and the Shinkansen.

Japan Best Time: Mar–May (Sakura) • Sep–Nov City • Culture • Tech

Quick Overview

Tokyo uniquely blends ultra-modern (skyscrapers, neon nightlife, electronics districts) with traditional culture (shrines, tea houses, seasonal festivals). It's efficient, endlessly discoverable and perfect for photographers, food-lovers and culture seekers.

Must-Visits

  • Shibuya Crossing & Hachiko
  • Shinjuku Nightlife & Omoide Yokocho
  • Senso-ji Temple (Asakusa)
  • Akihabara — anime & electronics
  • Tokyo Skytree & Tokyo Tower

Quick Tips

  • Get a Suica/Pasmo card for trains.
  • Use Google Maps + Hyperdia for JR timings.
  • Carry cash for small shops, but most accept cards.
  • Respect queueing and quiet carriage rules on trains.

3-Day Best Itinerary

Day 1 — Shibuya, Harajuku & Meiji Shrine

Cross the famous Shibuya scramble, stroll Takeshita Street (Harajuku), then find serenity at Meiji Jingu shrine. Evening: explore Omotesando cafes.

Day 2 — Asakusa, Ueno & Skytree

Visit Senso-ji temple in Asakusa, walk Ameya-Yokochō market (Ueno), then head to Tokyo Skytree for skyline views. Night: explore Akihabara.

Day 3 — Shinjuku & Nightlife

Morning in Shinjuku Gyoen (sakura season), afternoon in department stores, evening enjoy memory lanes (Omoide Yokocho) and neon bars in Kabukicho.

Food & Culture

  • Try sushi at a conveyor belt (kaiten) or a standing sushi bar.
  • Ramen alley experiences in Shinjuku and Ikebukuro.
  • Visit a traditional tea house or watch a kabuki show (book ahead).

How to Get Around

  • JR Yamanote line connects major hubs (Shibuya, Shinjuku, Ueno)
  • Purchase a Suica/Pasmo card for smooth travel
  • Use taxis for late-night returns (more expensive)

Map

Packing & Safety

  • Comfortable shoes for walking and trains.
  • Carry an umbrella — weather can change quickly.
  • Japan is very safe, but keep belongings secure in busy stations.
  • Learn basic Japanese greetings — locals appreciate effort.
